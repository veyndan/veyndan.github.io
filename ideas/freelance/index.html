Freelancing

What are the maximum number of hours I can freelance?

According to the Arbeitszeitgesetz (”Working Hours Act”), a working day is defined as Monday to Saturday, as one legally cannot work on a Sunday or public holiday. The maximum number of hours one can work per working day is 10 hours, averaging out at 48 hours per week over the course of 24 weeks.

§ 3 Working hours of employees The working hours of employees shall not exceed eight hours per working day. It may be extended to up to ten hours only if an average of eight hours per working day is not exceeded within six calendar months or within 24 weeks.
[…]
§ 9 Rest on Sundays and Public Holidays (1) Employees shall not be employed on Sundays and public holidays from 0:00 a.m. to midnight.

Conclusion

The maximum number of hours one can freelance is 10 hours per working day (Monday to Saturday), averaging out at 48 hours per week over the course of 24 weeks.

Freelance Only

Does my residency permit allow me to switch to freelancing?

According to BMI, I am able to switch over to being self-employed without notifying the authorities.

You can use the document to prove that you are residing in Germany legally and that you are allowed to work in Germany. For example, this allows you to switch from employment to self-employed economic activity or vice versa without having to notify or obtain permission from the foreigners authority.

I must, however, be able to support myself during this time, otherwise I risk losing my right of residence. Regaining my right of residence is not possible.

Loss of your right of residence is also possible if you no longer fulfil the requirements for it: for example if you do not yet have a right of permanent residence, if you lose your job, if you have not found a new job within six months, if there is no reasonable prospect that you will find a job in Germany and if you do not have sufficient resources to support yourself.
[…]
If you have lost your right of residence under the Withdrawal Agreement, you cannot regain it.

Conclusion

Yes, my residency permit allows me to switch to freelancing. However, I must be able to support myself during this time, otherwise I risk losing my right of residence.
