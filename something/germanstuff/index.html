Grammatical Gender in German

May 15 2022

Learning genders for German nouns is hard. Many rules can be found on the internet to help learn these nouns, but none of them tell you how valid a rule is (as like most grammar rules in any language — there are always exceptions). Below we state gender grammar rules, and give a percentage of how often this rules holds true.

Data is obtained through Wikidata, with links to each rule's query displayed. You can also click through to the full list of words that follow and don't follow the rules. If you notice a word is missing, please enter it here. If you notice a word is incorrect (e.g., wrong gender), then please correct it. To add or correct a rule, please check out an existing entry and you can follow its structure. Otherwise, please feel free to reach out to me. If you notice that a rule can be modified to increase the validity, or there is a rule that you think should be added, please reach out to me.

Rule Gender Validity
The word ends in -ig and is not a number. Masculine 100%
The word ends in -ast. Feminine 94%
The word ends in -ant but there is also this other rule and even another one. Neuter 94%
The word is a female job description. Feminine 72%
The word is a female family member. Feminine 72%
