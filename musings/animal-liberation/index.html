Animal Liberation

I've been a lifelong vegetarian, and have been vegan since 2013. I'm a vegan for purely ethical reasons. I've always been a proponent of animal welfare, yet have never done anything meaningful towards it, apart from changing my own habits. I'd like to do more.

Scope

Liberating all animals from the unnecessary suffering that humanity imparts is obviously too large a goal for a single person. Trapping and transferring the occasional spider from my living room to outside is obviously too small a goal. I want something somewhere in the middle.

I will focus on the UK dairy industry

How can I help significantly as an individual?

Doing something that meaningfully reduces the amount of animal suffering has always been elusive to me, but the industry is so large so I feel like a small fish, and is so out of my areas of expertise that I don't know how my skill set can help combat it. Usually when there is a problem I'm faced with, I can usually reduce it down to a problem that can be solved through programming, and hence actually have a go at solving it. To reduce the size of the animal agriculture industry, the only programming related things I believe could help are making websites for animal liberation groups for free. This isn't the 90s, and most non-technical people should be able to get a basic website up and running via any number of platforms. Not super helpful.

My passionate plea to do something helpful was recently reignited after watching Cow. In a similar vein to the film, instead of focusing on the whole industry, perhaps I should narrow my focus. Similarly again, myself having been brought up in Somerset, around mostly dairy farms, I feel that it'd be natural to focus my attention on the UK dairy industry. To be able to noticeably reduce the consumption of dairy within the UK would be a great task.

I could obviously go out in the streets and pass around fliers, pleading people to stop consuming dairy. I'm not trying to bash these people, but I individually want to make a bigger difference. In order to do so, I feel that I need to deeply understand the industry. It's inner workings. It's trends. It's dark secrets.

In order to do this, I want to first ask some probing questions about the industry, and then hopefully be able to answer them.

What should I focus on?

Counterintuitively, I believe that to have a significant effect, I must focus on a specific area of the business.

Why are you only focusing on the one country's dairy industry?

To have a deep understanding of a single country's dairy industry is a huge undertaking for a single person. To have a deep understanding of the dairy industry for multiple countries' is an astronomical task. Realistically, I'd end up having a shallow understanding of all the countries' in question.

Why aren't you focusing on another country?

I was born and raised in England, so it feels like a natural choice. I am currently living in Germany, but I'm not a citizen of Germany, nor do I speak German, so it'd be a much harder endeavour. Focusing on any other country wouldn't have the same immediacy to me.
