German Noun Genders

German nouns are gendered, so they can be feminine, masculine, or neuter. As an extremely novice German speaker, I often find remembering the word easier than which gender it is.

I'd like some rules to remember in order to increase the likelihood of guessing the correct gender for a word. Rules found around the web are great, yet I have no idea how accurate they are.

I've collected (and made up) several rules below, all of which have been tested against Wikidata to deduce their accuracy. I hope they can help you better remember the genders of nouns, and know that the rules you're learning are actually based in fact!

Rule Gender Total Words Total Words Where Rule Is Correct Percentage Correct
ends with -ant Masculine 36 32 89%
ends with -ich Masculine 64 57 89%
ends with -ig and isn't a number Masculine 33 33 100%
ends with …ismus Masculine 55 55 100%
ends with …ling Masculine 28 24 86%
ends with …or Masculine 118 111 94%
ends with …us but doesn't end with …ismus Masculine 124 56 45%

Hope that helped!
